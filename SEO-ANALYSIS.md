**Why it matters:** The title and description are what show in Google results. Using “bricks” and “bricks transport” here helps both ranking and click-through.

### 1.2 Content (brief explanations + keywords)

- **Hero:** Main headline and subtitle focus on “Bricks Transport” and “red bricks delivery” from Tamil Nadu & Kerala.
- **Services:** Each service has a short, clear explanation and uses “bricks transport”, “bricks delivery”, city names (Chennai, Coimbatore, Bangalore, Kochi), and “red bricks”.

**Why it matters:** Google ranks pages that clearly answer what people search for. Your content is written so that searches like “bricks”, “bricks transport”, “red bricks delivery Tamil Nadu” can match your page.

### 1.3 Structured data (JSON-LD schema)

Your site has four schema types in the HTML:

1. **Organization** – Who you are (Bricks Transport), contact, area served.
2. **WebSite** – That your site is about bricks transport and how users can get a quote.
3. **LocalBusiness** – Name, description, phone, email, area served, service type (bricks transport, red bricks delivery).
4. **FAQPage** – All 5 FAQ questions and answers so Google can show them as rich results (expandable FAQs in search).

**Why it matters:** Schema helps Google understand your business and show rich results (e.g. FAQ, contact, local) which can get more clicks and better visibility for “bricks transport” and related queries.

### 1.4 Technical basics

- **Canonical URL** – Set so Google knows the main version of your page.
- **Open Graph & Twitter** – Good titles and descriptions for sharing; also reinforce “bricks transport” and “bricks delivery”.
- **Geo meta** – Tamil Nadu and Kerala so local SEO is supported.
- **Semantic HTML** – Sections, headings (H1, H2, H3), and landmarks so Google can understand structure and topics (bricks, services, FAQ, contact).

## 2. Why You Might Not Show First Yet (and what controls it)

Showing **first** or in **suggestions** for “bricks” or “bricks transport” depends on:

1. **Domain and history** – New or low-authority sites rarely rank #1 quickly.
2. **Competition** – Many sites already target “bricks” and “bricks transport”; you need to be better or more relevant.
3. **Backlinks** – Other good sites linking to you (e.g. directories, construction portals, local business sites) improve trust and rankings.
4. **User behaviour** – If people click your result and stay (or call you), Google tends to show you more.
5. **Location** – Google shows different results by city/state; local relevance (Tamil Nadu, Kerala, South India) is already supported on your site.

So: **your page is already optimised for “bricks” and “bricks transport”**. To actually appear first or in suggestions, you need the actions below (off-page and ongoing work).

## 3. What You Need to Do to Get First Page / Suggestions for "Bricks" & "Bricks Transport"

### 3.1 Get your site on Google

- Use **Google Search Console** (search.google.com/search-console).
- Add your property: `https://brickstransport.com` (or your real domain).
- Submit the sitemap (e.g. `https://brickstransport.com/sitemap.xml` if you add one).
- Use “URL Inspection” to submit your homepage and important URLs.

Without this, Google may not index you properly, so you won’t show for “bricks” or “bricks transport” at all.

### 3.2 Use a proper domain and hosting

- Use a **real domain** (e.g. brickstransport.com, brickstransport.in) and **HTTPS**.
- Replace all `https://brickstransport.com` in meta tags and schema with your actual URL.
- Host the site on a fast, stable server (speed and uptime matter for SEO).

### 3.3 Add a sitemap and robots.txt

- **Sitemap:** Create `sitemap.xml` with your live URLs (at least homepage, and any other pages). Submit it in Search Console.
- **robots.txt:** In the `public` folder we added a `robots.txt` that allows Google and points to the sitemap. After build, ensure `robots.txt` is available at `https://yourdomain.com/robots.txt`.

This helps Google discover and prioritise your “bricks transport” page.

### 3.4 Build backlinks (most important for ranking first)

- List **Bricks Transport** on:
  - Google Business Profile (mandatory for local “bricks” / “bricks transport” searches).
  - JustDial, Sulekha, IndiaMART, and other local/directory sites.
  - Construction or building material directories that allow “bricks” or “bricks transport” as service.
- Get links from:
  - Supplier/kiln partner sites.
  - Builder/contractor associations or portals.
  - Local business or “best brick suppliers/transport” lists.

Use your **exact target phrases** in listings: e.g. “Bricks Transport – Red Bricks Delivery Tamil Nadu Kerala”.

### 3.5 Google Business Profile (for “bricks transport” near me)

- Create/claim **Google Business Profile** for Bricks Transport.
- Category: e.g. “Building Materials Supplier” or “Freight Transport”.
- Add address (or service area: Tamil Nadu, Kerala, Karnataka, Andhra Pradesh).
- Add phone, website (your site URL), and a short description rich in “bricks”, “bricks transport”, “red bricks delivery”.
- Add photos (trucks, bricks, delivery).
- Collect genuine reviews; reply to them.

This directly affects how you show for “bricks transport near me” and local “bricks” searches.

### 3.6 Keep content updated and add more over time

- Add a **blog or “Articles”** section: e.g. “How to order bricks for construction”, “Red bricks vs fly ash bricks”, “Bricks transport cost in Tamil Nadu”. Use “bricks” and “bricks transport” naturally.
- Add **location pages** if you serve many cities: e.g. “Bricks delivery Chennai”, “Bricks transport Coimbatore”. Each page should have a brief explanation and a clear CTA.
- Update the **FAQ** when you get new common questions (e.g. pricing, minimum order, payment terms) and keep schema in sync.

More quality content + keywords = more ways to rank for “bricks” and “bricks transport”.

### 3.7 Speed and mobile

- Keep the site **fast** (Angular is already optimised with production build).
- Ensure it works well on **mobile** (your layout is responsive). Google uses mobile-first indexing.
